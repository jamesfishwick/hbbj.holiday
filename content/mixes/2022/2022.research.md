## 1. Snoopy's Christmas - The Royal Guardsmen

**Release Info:**
- Released 1967
- Peaked at #1 in New Zealand and Australia
- Written by Phil Gernhard and Dick Holler

**Song Story:**
- Follow-up to their 1966 hit "Snoopy vs. the Red Baron"
- Fictional story of Snoopy and the Red Baron's dogfight ending in a Christmas Eve toast
- The song depicts the Red Baron sparing Snoopy on Christmas Eve and forcing him to land behind enemy lines where they share a holiday toast

**Artist Info:**
- American rock band from Ocala, Florida
- Known for Peanuts-themed novelty songs

**Links:**
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snoopy%27s_Christmas

## 2. Ringing Bells (Sweet Music), Pt. 2 - Young Senators

**Release Info:**
- Originally released early 1970s on Innovation label
- Reissued in 2016 (first legitimate release in 45 years)

**Song Story:**
- **NOTE: This is NOT a Christmas song** - the "ringing bells" in the title caused confusion
- Described as "a sought after funky, Psychedelic 'GO GO' classic"
- Exemplary progenitor of DC's hyper-local, hyper-celebrated Go-Go scene

**Artist Info:**
- DC-based septet led by energetic singer and conga player Jimi Dougans
- Featured: Leroy Flemming (sax), Calvin Charisty (guitar), Frank Hooker (keyboards), James Johnson (drums), Wornell Jones (bass), Phillip Guilbeau (trumpet)
- Band issued two singles on the tiny Innovation label before joining up with Motown's Eddie Kendricks in 1972 for his sophomore effort People…Hold On

**Links:**
- https://numerogroup.com/products/the-young-senators-ringing-bells-sweet-music-part-1-bw-part-2

## 4. Stop The Cavalry - Jona Lewie

**Release Info:**
- Released November 1980
- Peaked at #3 on UK Singles Chart
- Originally wasn't intended as a Christmas song

**Song Story:**
- Anti-war song about a soldier wanting to go home
- The only reference to Christmas is the single line "I wish I was at home for Christmas"
- Record company added tubular bells to make it sound more Christmassy
- Has become a perennial Christmas favorite despite not being written as one

**Artist Info:**
- British singer-songwriter and multi-instrumentalist
- Known for quirky, theatrical pop songs

**Links:**
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stop_the_Cavalry

## 8. I Want To Come Home For Christmas - Long Version - Marvin Gaye

**Song Story:**
- Written about a Vietnam War prisoner of war wanting to come home for Christmas

- Remained unreleased until after Gaye's death in 1984
- One of the most poignant anti-war Christmas songs ever recorded

**Artist Info:**

## 17. Santa Claus Blues - Black Ace

**Release Info:**
- Recorded as "Christmas Time Blues (Beggin' Santa Claus)"

**Song Story:**
- Texas blues guitarist's take on Christmas blues
- Born December 21, 1907 (just before Christmas)

**Artist Info:**
- Black Ace (real name: Babe Kyro Lemon Turner)
- Born December 21, 1907, Hughes Springs, Texas
- Died November 7, 1972, Fort Worth, Texas
- Also known as B.K. Turner, Black Ace Turner or Babe Turner
- Raised on family farm, taught himself guitar
- Bought National steel guitar and played "Hawaii meets the Delta" - smooth and simple blues
- Recorded six songs for Chicago's Decca Records in Dallas in 1937
- Started radio show in Fort Worth in 1937

## 19. The Loneliest Christmas Tree - Lisa Miller

**Release Info:**
- Issued as single late 1967 on Canterbury label
- B-side to "Love Is"

**Song Story:**
- Lisa Miller was only 10 or 11 years old when recorded
- Her voice was far more mature than her years
- Most material written by her mother, Kay Lewis, and her aunt, Helen Lewis
- In 1967, Miller recorded about an album's worth of material for Canterbury
- Small quantity of promotional LPs were pressed but not released
- Recordings finally appeared on 2010 Sundazed CD "Within Myself"
- 1967 single added as bonus cuts to the 2010 release

**Links:**
- https://www.allmusic.com/song/the-loneliest-christmas-tree-mt0038706647
- https://lisamiller-sd.bandcamp.com/track/the-loneliest-christmas-tree
