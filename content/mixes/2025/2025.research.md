## 1. Zat You Santa Claus - Buster Poindexter And His Banshees Of Blue

**Release Info:**
- Released 1987 on RCA
- Produced by Hank Medress
- Cover of Louis Armstrong's 1953 original
- Written by Jack Fox

**Song Story:**
- Louis Armstrong's original released in 1953
- Buster Poindexter is the pseudonym rock singer David Johansen adopted in the mid-'80s
- Johansen performed at New York club Tramps in a tuxedo with his band The Banshees of Blue
- The act featured pop standards, jump blues, and novelty material
- Song captures childlike anticipation of Christmas Eve

**Artist Info:**
- David Johansen was former frontman of the New York Dolls
- Created the Buster Poindexter persona for semi-comic nightclub performances
- Notable renditions of this song also by Harry Connick Jr. and Brian Setzer

**Links:**
- https://www.whosampled.com/cover/430186/Buster-Poindexter-His-Banshees-of-Blue-Zat-You,-Santa-Claus-Louis-Armstrong-Zat-You,-Santa-Claus%3F/

## 2. Esta noche es Nochebuena - Los Chunguitos

**Release Info:**
- Released 1984
- From album "Navidades Flamencas. Villancicos con Duende"
- Tagged as rumba, Spanish, Christmas

**Song Story:**
- Opens with "Esta noche es Nochebuena / y mañana Navidad" - the traditional Spanish villancico "La Marimorena"
- Rumba flamenco arrangement of traditional Christmas material

**Artist Info:**
- Started by Juan, Manuel, and Jose Salazar - three brothers from Vallecas barrio of Madrid
- Born of gitano (gypsy/roma) heritage
- One of the famous rumba groups of the '70s and '80s
- Along with Los Chicos, helped popularize rumba flamenco using modern instrumentation

**Links:**
- https://www.last.fm/music/Los+Chunguitos/_/Esta+Noche+Es+Nochebuena
- https://open.spotify.com/track/6pC1M6oEqPwZ7qtVoAsMYm
