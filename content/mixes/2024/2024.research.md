## 1. Santa's Beard - They Might Be Giants

**Release Info:**
- First released on album "Lincoln"
- Later appeared on 2001 holiday EP "Holidayland"

**Song Story:**
- Title came from a Watchface performance piece called "Woolworth's" that catalogued items you could buy at Woolworth's stores
- Band members were struck by a character yelling out "Santa beards!"
- John Flansburgh later discovered there was also a Beach Boys song called "Santa's Beard"

**Song Meaning:**
- Darkly humorous take on holiday infidelity
- References "I Saw Mommy Kissing Santa Claus" but as an actual love triangle
- About a narrator who becomes jealous when his wife/girlfriend shows affection toward his friend who dresses up as Santa each year

**Links:**
- https://tmbw.net/wiki/Santa's_Beard (TMBG Wiki with detailed info)
- https://tmbw.net/wiki/Interpretations:Santa's_Beard (fan interpretations)
- https://tmbg.bandcamp.com/track/santas-beard-2

## 14. Santa's Got a GTO - The Ramonas

**Release Info:**
- First released as cassette tape for fan club in 1990
- Later released as A-side of 7" single
- Featured on multiple "Santa's Got a GTO" compilations curated by Rodney Bingenheimer

**Song Story:**
- The Ramonas claimed to be "the first all-female Ramones tribute band in the world"
- Formed in San Francisco in 1987
- Good friends who loved the Ramones, they literally drew straws to see who would play what instrument since none of them were musicians
- Gained popularity when Dr. Demento and Rodney Bingenheimer started playing it
- Rodney Bingenheimer fell in love with the track and has played it on his show every year at Christmas time since it first came out

**Band History:**
- Performed in San Francisco and Bay Area from 1987 through summer 1991
- Invited by Joey Ramone to play the official RAMONES website launch CyberPunk Blitz at CBGB New York City, Dec 17, 1999
- Disbanded permanently after the CBGB show

**Links:**
- https://gearhead.bandcamp.com/track/santas-got-a-gto
- https://hipchristmas.com/albums/r/rodney.php
- https://gearhead.bandcamp.com/album/rodney-on-the-rock-presents-santas-got-a-gto-vol-2

## 16. Jingle Bells - Roger Sprung

**Artist Info:**
- Roger Sprung (August 29, 1930 – July 22, 2023)
- American banjo player and teacher
- Best known for introducing authentic bluegrass banjo picking styles to the folk music community in the north

- Known for eclectic adaptation of bluegrass banjo techniques to other genres
- Performances cover everything from "Jingle Bells" and "Puff the Magic Dragon" to "Greensleeves" and "It's A Small World"

## 18. baby jesus is nobody's baby now - Julia Jacklin

**Release Info:**
- Written and released 2019

**Song Story:**
- Written while stuck in smoke-covered Melbourne during Australian bushfires
- Jacklin's family in the country faced direct threat from fires
- Roads were blocked and family were being evacuated periodically for a month
- Melbourne was blanketed in smoke from the fires
- She said "2019 was a pretty rough one for my family" and "I was touring the whole year carrying a lot of guilt for not being able to be at home"
- Imagined Christmas as a time when everyone would come together and take a collective breath, but then the bushfires hit
- Wrote the song in her room looking forward to 2020, hoping it would be a reset

**Song Meaning:**
- Described as depicting a hellish family gathering
- Lyrics allude to a miscarriage, or a breakup, or both
- Chronicles a quiet and desperate form of depression
- Deals with themes of family tension, loss, loneliness, and trauma from Australian bushfire crisis

## 24. December Skies - Steven Wilson

**Release Info:**
- Released exclusively on YouTube December 14, 2023
- Physical editions (CD and vinyl) released December 6, 2024
- Two collector's edition formats: gatefold 7" vinyl and CD digifile, both with 3 exclusive Christmas cards

**Song Story:**
- Steven Wilson of Porcupine Tree's first ever Christmas song
- Written in collaboration with ChatGPT AI
- Wilson's friend suggested using ChatGPT to generate Christmas lyrics in his style
- Wilson guided the AI with instructions like "don't actually mention Christmas" and "make it feel more lonely"
- Features gorgeous slide guitar work from Randy McStine (who joined Wilson on two recent Porcupine Tree tours)

**Video:**
- Created with AI by Miles Skarin
- Based on imagery from Wilson's previous videos placed into 2D and 3D backdrops

**Artwork:**
- Christmas cards and cover illustrated by Hajo Müller

**Links:**
- https://stevenwilsonhq.com/december-skies/
