## 3. The Three Suns - Ding Dong Dandy Christmas

**Release Info:**
- Album "A Ding Dong Dandy Christmas!" released in 1959
- Genre: Christmas, Exotica, Holidays, Lounge, Traditional Pop, Vocal Pop

**Artist Info:**
- American pop group formed in 1939
- Most popular during 1940s and 1950s
- Brothers Al Nevins (guitar) and Morty Nevins (accordion) with cousin Artie Dunn (vocals, electronic organ)

**Album Details:**
- Features melodic lines on accordion, guitar, tuba, and organ with hip percussion
- Original liner notes boast addition of two tubas, chimes, bells and an oboe
- Used RCA Victor sessions as audio laboratory, employing stereophonic effects
- Long cherished by Christmas music devotees and lounge music fans

## 17. Arthur Lyman - Christmas Song

**Release Info:**
- Album: "Mele Kalikimaka (Merry Christmas)" (1963)
- Reissued on CD as "With A Christmas Vibe" (Rykodisc, 1996)
- Includes "The Christmas Song" plus 11 other holiday standards
- Duration: 2:38

**Artist Info:**
- American jazz vibraphone and marimba player
- Popularized faux-Polynesian music during 1950s-1960s (later known as exotica)
- Among exotica artists of that era, only Lyman recorded a Christmas album

**Album Details:**
- Instrumental versions of holiday hymns and secular favorites
- Vibraphonic treatment that is exotic and soothing
- Features trademark vibes and marimba
- Hawaiian instruments (ukulele, steel guitar)
- Colorful sound effects (shrieking macaws, rolling surf)

## 20. Blur - The Wassailing Song

**Release Info:**
- Recorded 1992 during break in "Modern Life Is Rubbish" album sessions
- Released as limited edition single-sided 7" December 15, 1992
- Attributed to fictitious band "Gold, Frankincense and Blur"
- Only 500 copies pressed
- Given away after Blur's gig at Hibernian Club in Fulham by man dressed as Santa

**Song Story:**
- "The Gloucestershire Wassail"
- Damon Albarn and Graham Coxon introduced to song by music teacher Nigel Hildreth at Stanway Comprehensive School, Essex in early 1980s
- Only song in Blur discography that drummer Dave Rowntree sings on
- Four members alternate vocals on verses, singing refrain together

**Collectibility:**
- Rare collector's item

**Links:**

- https://www.discogs.com/release/541389-Gold-Frankincense-And-Blur-The-Wassailing-Song
